Death

Chapter 1 - Introduction

The Forgotten Story

Once upon a time, long before anyone could remember, our world was very different.

 

Everything was cold in our world. The stars stopped moving, the planets stopped rotating, the sun stopped shining. Everything felt like it had lost all of its hope. Everything just wanted to die.

 

There were only two beings left in this plane of existence, filled with boredom, looking at the never moving sky in the night.

 

"Tell me a story!" - said one spirit to the other.

 

The other spirit, hearing those words felt happy. Something he had never felt for a while.

 

He started his story...

 

“I am going to tell you a story that even time itself may have forgotten. This is the story of the people whose footsteps have long been vanished. This is the story of our people.

 

Going down from the stars, from all of the sky’s terror, tearing through the clouds, you’d finally see the beautiful city of Eohm, the capital of this entire planet. The capital was home for more than twenty billion people, it was immense, and yet, it was built so carefully.

 

The civilians had no need to be in a rush, they had all the time in the world, quite literally. Their immortality made them precise workers, but made their life incredibly boring.

 

After living for so much time, they had lost all interest in everything, from creation, from love and from hate. What was the point in seeking the mystery of an afterlife if their life would never end?

 

They felt like their life had no meaning. Things were just repeating all over and over again in front of their eyes. People being born, growing up and then leaving to go to work, all of this for hundreds of thousands of generations. What was the point of being born in this meaningless world? 

 

Just to live? For what?

 

For this reason, people slowly stopped procreating, thinking it would be immoral to bring any new life on this miserable world. And for many other aeons, no child was ever seen running around the city’s square, or anywhere else in the world.

 

That is, until Yin came into life. The last child born in this meaningless suffering.

The Marble Temple

Eohm was the capital of our world. In it, there was a giant white temple, made out of marble, with columns surrounding the walls, much like one of those greek ruins.

 

The sunrays made the temple shine with incredible beauty, stealing the spotlight in everyone’s sight, it was a magnet for the eyes. But the true beauty lied inside.

 

To enter, you had to go through one of the nine pair of doors surrounding it, three doors on each side, apart from the back, which had none. The doors were about 4 meters tall and 2 meters wide each. They were made out of an incredible strong metal that was very light in weight, but so dense that no particle of light could pass through it.

 

Therefore, the inside of the temple was purely dark if no internal source of light was involved. 

 

And yet, there was light.

 

Sixteen torches surrounded the four walls, four on each side. Those torches were quite mind-bogglind, they never stopped burning, their fire, forever shining from its ashes. It was so beautiful to look at. But the torches light didn’t light up the entire temple, the middle was still dark.

 

The temple was quite immense on the inside, and yet it was not empty at all.

Four Walls

The forever-burning torches in the temple revealed mysterious paintings on the walls, feeling almost as if they were sacred. Who could have painted those masterpieces, so perfect, yet so intriguing?

 

On each wall, you could see a story, each one being some sort of continuation of the previous one.

 

If you’d place yourself in the middle of the temple and make a full turn, you would see a cycle of an eternal story, always ending, always starting again, as if nothing happened.

 

It felt like it described a cycle of creation that could not be broken, even if someone wanted to. As if the Creator’s hands were tied to follow this cycle, as if he himself was cursed, with no choice.

 

The wall opposite to the main entrance was painted with dark colours, close to black but with some drops of red. No human figures were present on any of the four walls, just colours and feelings.

 

The walls were painted with feelings. Feelings so powerful they could be materialized. 

 

This first wall injected a feeling of agony and despair inside the seeker’s eyes. It would leave him with a sense of hopelessness, sadness, as if everything was lost, as if everything had no meaning, no value.

 

Looking at your left, you’d see the next wall in the temple’s cycle. Every wall was connected to its neighbours, the paintings, bounded, their colours, their feelings, were spread on the adjacent ones.

 

This wall had lighter colours, like a rise from the ashes from the previous one. It was painted in red, green and blue, the primary colours from which anything could be created. This painting left the feeling of hope, of creation, of a new beginning. A rise from the ashes in which it was burning in before.

 

The wall where the main entrance was situated, from which every human would enter, was painted with warm and light colours, such as orange and yellow. This painting left a feeling of curiosity, of joy, of discovery. Anybody that would have looked at this painting would be filled with a feeling of wanting to explore the temple, to know more, to know how things worked.

 

This represented the joy the Creator must have felt while carefully putting together His masterpiece.

 

Looking at your right felt like looking into the future. The painting on the last wall was filled with red, the colour of fury. This painting represented anger, destruction, regret. It felt like a terrible ending to something so beautiful. An evitable ending. Nothing could stop time, not love, not fury, not even the Creator.
